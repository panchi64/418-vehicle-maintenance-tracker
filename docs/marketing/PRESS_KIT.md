# Press Kit

> Everything a journalist, reviewer, or podcast producer needs to write about Checkpoint without emailing the developer back. Hosted at `checkpoint.franciscocasiano.com/press` (route already exists). The downloadable bundle (`press-kit.zip`) is built from this content.

The single most quoted line of indie-press advice from Curtis Herbert applies here: *"It's all about finding the story — answer why their readers will care, not why your app is great."* ([blog.curtisherbert.com — An Indie's Guide to the Press](https://blog.curtisherbert.com/an-indies-guide-to-the-press/)). Everything below exists to make the journalist's job of finding their story easier.

## 4. Five story angles (the journalist's catalog)

A press contact will skim a kit and pick *the angle that fits their beat*. Pre-write the angles. This is the single most-quoted piece of indie press advice. ([Curtis Herbert](https://blog.curtisherbert.com/an-indies-guide-to-the-press/))

| Angle | Beat / Outlet fit | The story in one sentence |
|---|---|---|
| **A. The local-dev story** | Sin Comillas, El Nuevo Día Negocios, NotiCel | A solo developer in PR builds a maintenance app that's localized for the marbete cycle the government no longer reminds you about. |
| **B. The anti-subscription story** | MacStories, The Sweet Setup, Indie Hackers | An iPhone app launches in 2026 with no subscription, no ads, no account — what does the math look like for a sustainable indie business? |
| **C. The Apple-ecosystem story** | MacStories, 9to5Mac, AppStories | A vehicle app implements every Apple surface — Widgets, Watch, Siri, CarPlay, Live Activities — in a way no competitor has. |
| **D. The data-loss / trust story** | Macworld, Six Colors, Apple Insider | Checkpoint's iCloud-only / no-account stance answers the #1 complaint in the vehicle-app category (data loss). |
| **E. The Puerto-Rico-first story** | Estamos en Línea, Bloomberg Línea PR, local podcasts | Why a Puerto Rico developer chose to launch in his home market first, and what's different about car ownership here. |

### Exclusive strategy (decision recorded)

Curtis Herbert's rule — *exclusivity to one outlet, not two* — applies within a competitive set, not across non-competing beats. PR press and US iOS press serve different audiences and don't read each other. So Checkpoint offers **two parallel, non-competing exclusives**:

1. ✅ **PR-press exclusive — locked: El Nuevo Día (Negocios section).** Local-dev story (Angle A). Pitched 7 days before the PR-market launch news embargo. If they pass within 72 hours, fall back to Sin Comillas, then NotiCel — same angle, no exclusivity offered.
2. ⚠️ **US-iOS-press exclusive — still open: MacStories vs The Sweet Setup.** Apple-ecosystem angle (C) for MacStories, anti-subscription angle (B) for Sweet Setup. Pick one based on whichever responds first to a casual pre-pitch ping ~6 weeks before launch. Don't pitch both; the loser becomes a Tier-2 batch outlet 7 days after the winner's piece ships.

The two exclusives are timed independently — El Nuevo Día is geared to the Phase 2 (late-July) PR spike, the US iOS exclusive is geared to Phase 1 soft launch.

## 8. Embargo & timing protocol

- **Default:** No embargo. Send the pitch when the app is approved and live in the App Store.
- **Exclusive offer:** "If you'd like an early look, I can send a TestFlight build and a 1-week embargo before the public launch date."
- **Embargo break clause:** Always include — *"if another outlet is about to publish anything that overlaps, I'll give you 24 hours notice to publish first."*
- **Never break an embargo offered to a competitor.** It will be your last pitch to that journalist.

## 9. The reviewer FAQ (anticipating what they'll ask)

Pre-write the answers. Drop into a private Notion doc; send the link on request.

1. **Who built it?** One person — Francisco Casiano, in PR.
2. **What's the business model?** Free for 3 vehicles; $9.99 one-time for unlimited. No subscription, no ads.
3. **Does it require a login or account?** No.
4. **What data does it collect?** Per the privacy nutrition label: purchases, coarse location (for seasonal reminders, optional), photos (for OCR), diagnostics (PostHog, opt-out). No PII linked to identity. Self-hosted analytics.
5. **Does it sync?** Yes, via iCloud / CloudKit. Free, native, no separate account.
6. **Does it work offline?** Fully. iCloud is additive.
7. **What's coming next?** Per the roadmap (`FEATURES.md`): EV support, fuel tracking, Live Activities, AI-OCR (subscription, eventually).
8. **Why no Android?** Apple ecosystem is the product's positioning, not a temporary state. It's a permanent choice.
9. **Why Puerto Rico first?** It's home, it's underserved by US-made apps, and the marbete cycle is a natural product hook.
10. **What's an in-app purchase vs the Pro one-time?** Pro = unlimited vehicles + 4-6 themes. Tips ($1.99/$4.99/$9.99) are separate, unlock random rare themes. Tip ≠ Pro.

## 10. Post-coverage follow-up

After any piece of coverage:

1. Within 24 hours, public "thank you" post on social tagging the outlet.
2. Update the website's `/press` page with the article (logo + quote + link).
3. Within 1 week, send a personal note (not a template) to the writer offering a follow-up if relevant.
4. Track the install bump via App Analytics referrer report. Note for future planning.
