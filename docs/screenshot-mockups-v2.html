How to Read These Mockups

The dark textured backgrounds simulate real photography.

In production, these will be actual photos from your mirrorless camera — garage workbench, owner's manual, car interior, etc. The CSS textures here are placeholders that approximate the tonal quality and mood of each scene.

The phone screens will be composited in post.

Photograph your phone with the screen off or showing a solid dark color. In Photoshop/Affinity, mask the screen area and perspective-warp a real app screenshot to fit. Add a subtle glass reflection for realism.

The blurred props tell the story.

The wrench, receipt, keys, and steering wheel are all out of focus — they provide context without competing. Your mirrorless at f/1.8–2.8 will naturally create this separation.

What competitors do

Flat device mockup floating on a gradient. Maybe a drop shadow. Caption in a system font. Looks like every other app in the category. Zero personality, zero context.

What Checkpoint does

A phone on a real workbench, screen glowing cerulean, wrench going soft in the background. Monospace caption above. It says: this app was made by someone who understands both cars and design. You feel it before you read a word.
